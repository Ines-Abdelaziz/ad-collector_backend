CheckMyNews Longitudinal Study 1

We need your help to monitor what information gets amplified on Facebook! You will be asked to install a monitoring tool we developed and keep it active for 6 weeks and participate in 5 surveys (that will altogether take less than 30 minutes to complete).

Who we are and what we do:

We are researchers from the MIAI Multidisciplinary Institute on Artificial Intelligence and the Max Planck Institute in Europe and the purpose of this study is to measure if there are systemic biases in the quality of content users receive on Facebook from news medias among different psycho-socio-demographic groups. You can see how our previous work pushed Facebook to make its platform safer and more transparent for users at the end of the study description.

Reward for your participation:

You will receive an initial reward of 0.75 pounds upon setting up the study (installing our tool, browsing your Facebook timeline a few moments and filling a short socio-demographic survey). You will get an additional 3.5 pounds bonus at the end of the study (in 6 weeks) if you filled the four additional surveys, and you kept our tool active on the browser where you check Facebook. At the end of the study, we will provide you with a personalized report that analyzes your answers to our surveys and the content from news medias you receive on Facebook.

Task details:

  1. Install our monitoring tool on Chrome using this link, read and accept the consent and keep the tool active for a period of 6 weeks.
  2. Fill out the 5 surveys (approx. one each week). A popup will appear when you have a new survey to complete. The popup is triggered by visiting Facebook and may be in the background. Otherwise you can access the surveys by clicking on Start Survey in the extension menu.
  3. Browse normally Facebook from the browser where you installed the tool.

What data we collect:

Our monitoring tool works silently in the background while you are browsing Facebook without any overhead to your browsing experience. The tool collects information about the content you RECEIVE on your timeline and does not collect information about the content you SHARE on Facebook. We collect data about posts you see on your timeline that are public or that refer to a news article (e.g., we collect the posts you see on your timeline that refer to articles on nytimes.com). We DO NOT collect your personal comments, messages or the photos and content you share with your friends. All data is pseudonymized and we do not send to our servers any personally identifiable information (e.g., your email, name, phone number). Please check the Privacy Policy for a detailed description of the data we collect. Our universities are very attentive and vigilant regarding personal data protection issues, and our monitoring tool has been verified by our universities and meets the highest standards in terms of security and privacy.

Thank you for your participation! Your input helps us understand systemic biases in information exposure online!
